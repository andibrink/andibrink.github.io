A Mockery: Light Pollution and Bird Deaths

  • At the base of the hotel,
  • a Sparrow lies on the concrete
  • in the orange light of dawn.
  • Smaller than your hand,
  • and much stiller.

  • She is reflected
  • in the cool blue glass
  • Her doppelganger silent
  • in the window of this new and
  • shining monument.
  • Its beauty claims another life,
  • another fifty.
  • Where Sweetgum
  • and Live Oaks
  • once stood, it glows.
  • Beckons in the bright, starless night
  • the refuse of our teeming shore.
  • The Dune Mice and Turtle Hatchlings
  • wander steadfast
  • towards that falsemoon.
  • Crawling,
  • Slowly,
  • Surely.
  • through the still-warm sand
  • and the smooth cordgrass.
  • Away from the steady beat of the waves,
  • and their mothers' worried hearts.
  • The dunes and beachgrass hum with the wind,
  • and the low sounds of the long, hot night
  • sing on.
